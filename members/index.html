Jimin Liang

Professor / Biomedical Engineering

Github Website Email

Jimin Liang received the B.E., M.E. and Ph. D. degrees in electronic engineering from Xidian University, Xi'an, China, in 1992, 1995 and 2000, respectively. He joined Xidian University in 1995, where he is now a professor in the School of Life Science and Technology. In the year of 2002, he was a research associate professor at the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department, University of Tennessee, Knoxville, USA. He is an executive member of Shaanxi Society of Biomedical Engineering, standing member of the Molecular Imaging Society of China, and member of IEEE and SPIE. His general areas of research are in biomedical imaging, medical image analysis and pattern recognition. He has authored or coauthored more than 100 journal papers and 30 granted patents on those topics. He was the recipient of 2010 Progress Award in Science and Technology of the Ministry of Education of China and 2012 National Invention Award of China.

Minghao Dong

Associate Professor / Neural Imaging

Website Email

Minghao Dong received the B.E., M.E. and Ph. D. degrees in electronic engineering from Xidian University, Xi'an, China. He received the ‘Outstanding junior researcher award in basic science’ from Society of Acupuncture Research in 2013. His general areas of research are in cognitive neuroscience and hybrid artificial intelligence. Specifically, his study concerns how real-world visual experience shapes the human brain and how these plastic brain changes facilitate human performance (especially visual experience) in return using MRI, EEG and behavioral methods. Also, he focuses on how acquisition of real-world behavioral expertise (i.e. visual expertise) can be accelerated through behavioral training and non-invasive brain stimulation. Moreover, he is particularly interested in how visual categorization and recognition can be optimized by hybrid intelligent system combining human visual recognition expertise and machine capability.
